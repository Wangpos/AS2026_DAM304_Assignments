1. Overview
This assignment brings together Units IV and V . You will design and pre-train a GPT-style
language model of your own specification, applying at least two Unit V optimization techniques,
and evaluate it by generating text and computing perplexity. The assignment is assessed
independently and you make all architectural and training decisions and must justify them.
Module Descriptor Alignment
LO4: Design and construct core components of an LLM architecture.
LO5: Develop effective pre-training strategies for large language models.
Covers Units IV (4.1–4.3) and V (5.1–5.3). Assessed out of 15 marks.
2. Assignment Tasks
Part 1 : Architecture Design and Justification (25 marks)
1. Build a decoder-only GPT-style model in PyTorch (no HuggingFace model classes).
Constraints: 1–15 million parameters; pre-layer normalisation; causal masking; sinusoidal
or learned positional encoding; tied embedding/LM head weights.
2. Complete an architecture decision table in your report for: d_model, num_heads,
num_layers, d_ff, max_seq_len, and vocab_size — with a one-sentence justification for
each choice.
3. Verify: forward pass on (2, 64) input produces logits of shape (2, 64, vocab_size). Print
parameter breakdown: embedding, per-block, LM head, total.

4. Train on a text corpus of your choice (minimum 200KB). Describe source, size, and

Part 3 : Text Generation and Evaluation (35 marks)
7. Implement all three decoding strategies in a single generate(model, prompt, strategy,
**kwargs) function: greedy, top-k (configurable k), and top-p (configurable p and
temperature).
8. Generate 150 tokens from two prompts with all three strategies. Present in a table
(prompt | strategy | output).
9. Temperature ablation: generate 3 outputs each at temperatures 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 using
top-p. Discuss the coherence-diversity trade-off in your report.
10. Error analysis: identify three failure modes of your model. For each, propose a concrete
fix.
